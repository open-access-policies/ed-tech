# Breach Notification (Schools) Policy (RES-POL-003)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s requirements for notifying schools, districts, and regulatory authorities in the event of a data breach involving student data. It ensures compliance with FERPA, COPPA, state student privacy laws, and contractual notification obligations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All confirmed or suspected breaches involving student data
- All notification obligations to schools, districts, parents, and regulators
- All workforce members involved in incident response and notification
- All student data regardless of format or location

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** promptly notifies affected schools of any security incident involving their student data, enabling schools to meet their own notification obligations and take protective action.

### 3.1 Breach Definition

**3.1.1 What Constitutes a Breach**

For purposes of this policy, a breach includes:
- Unauthorized access to student data
- Unauthorized acquisition of student data
- Unauthorized disclosure of student data
- Loss of student data (including lost devices or media)
- Ransomware or malware affecting student data systems
- Any incident requiring notification under applicable law

**3.1.2 Breach vs. Security Incident**

| Classification | Definition | Notification Required |
|----------------|------------|----------------------|
| **Security Incident** | Any event potentially affecting security | Internal only (unless escalated) |
| **Suspected Breach** | Incident under investigation that may involve unauthorized access | School notification if likely breach |
| **Confirmed Breach** | Verified unauthorized access/acquisition/disclosure | School notification required |

### 3.2 Notification Timelines

**3.2.1 School Notification**

**[Company Name]** notifies affected schools within the following timelines:

| Notification Type | Timeline | Trigger |
|-------------------|----------|---------|
| **Initial notification** | Within **[Number, e.g., 24]** hours | Suspected or confirmed breach discovered |
| **Updated notification** | Within **[Number, e.g., 48]** hours | Additional information available |
| **Final report** | Within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days | Investigation complete |

**Note:** DPA terms may specify different timelines; the shorter timeline applies.

**3.2.2 State-Specific Timelines**

Some states have specific notification timelines:

| State | Notification Timeline | Additional Requirements |
|-------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **New York** | Promptly; within timeframe specified in 8 NYCRR 121.9 | Notify school and NY Chief Privacy Officer |
| **California** | Most expedient time possible | Notify CA Attorney General if >500 residents |
| **Colorado** | Within 30 days | Notify CO Attorney General |
| **Connecticut** | Within 60 days | Notify CT Attorney General |
| **Texas** | Within 60 days | Notify TX Attorney General if >250 residents |

See ANNEX-003 (State Privacy Law Matrix) for complete state requirements.

**3.2.3 Regulatory Notification**

If required by law or DPA:
- Federal regulators (FTC for COPPA violations)
- State Attorneys General
- State education departments
- Other regulatory bodies as required

### 3.3 Notification Content

**3.3.1 Initial Notification**

Initial notification to schools includes (to the extent known):
- Date/time breach was discovered
- Nature of the incident
- Types of student data potentially affected
- Number of students potentially affected
- Status of investigation
- Immediate steps being taken
- Point of contact for questions

**3.3.2 Updated Notification**

As investigation proceeds:
- Additional findings
- Refined scope (students, data types)
- Root cause (if determined)
- Remediation actions taken
- Timeline for final report

**3.3.3 Final Report**

Complete report includes:
- Summary of incident
- Timeline of events
- Root cause analysis
- Data types and number of individuals affected
- Remediation actions completed
- Steps to prevent recurrence
- Recommendations for schools (if applicable)

### 3.4 Notification Process

**3.4.1 Internal Escalation**

When a potential breach is identified:
1. Security team assesses incident severity
2. Incident classified per RES-POL-001
3. Student Data Privacy Officer notified immediately for student data incidents
4. Legal/Compliance notified
5. Executive leadership notified for significant incidents

**3.4.2 School Notification Execution**

| Step | Responsibility | Timeline |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Draft notification | Incident Response Team | Within hours of confirmation |
| Review notification | Legal/Compliance + Privacy Officer | Before sending |
| Approve notification | **[Role, e.g., CISO or General Counsel]** | Before sending |
| Send notification | Customer Success or Privacy Officer | Per policy timeline |
| Document notification | Incident Response Team | Upon sending |

**3.4.3 Communication Channels**

Notifications sent via:
- Email to DPA-designated security contact
- Phone call for urgent notifications
- Secure portal message (if available)
- Written letter (for formal notification, if required)

**3.4.4 Documentation**

Maintain records of:
- All notifications sent (content, recipient, method, timestamp)
- School acknowledgments received
- Follow-up communications
- Final resolution documentation

### 3.5 Supporting Schools

**3.5.1 Enabling School Notifications**

**[Company Name]** provides schools with information to support their notification obligations:
- Substitute notification services (if schools choose to outsource)
- Template notification language (if requested)
- Data to populate state notification forms
- Technical assistance for parent communication

**3.5.2 School's Notification Responsibilities**

Under FERPA, schools (not vendors) are responsible for:
- Notifying parents of breach
- Maintaining record of disclosure
- Determining appropriate protective actions

**[Company Name]** supports but does not replace school obligations.

**3.5.3 Parent Communication**

If school authorizes **[Company Name]** to communicate directly with parents:
- Obtain explicit authorization in writing
- Follow school-approved messaging
- Provide school visibility into communications
- Document all parent contacts

### 3.6 Regulatory Notification

**3.6.1 State Attorney General Notification**

Many states require vendor or school notification to the Attorney General:

| State | AG Notification Threshold | Who Notifies |
|-------|--------------------------|--------------|
| **California** | >500 CA residents | School or vendor |
| **New York** | Per Education Law 2-d | Vendor + School |
| **Texas** | >250 TX residents | School |
| **Most states** | Varies by state | School (vendor supports) |

**3.6.2 FTC Notification (COPPA)**

For breaches involving children under 13:
- Assess whether FTC notification is required
- Consult with Legal on COPPA implications
- Prepare for potential FTC inquiry

**3.6.3 Other Regulators**

Notify additional regulators if:
- Required by DPA or contract
- Required by state education department
- Required by federal funding conditions

### 3.7 Breach Response Coordination

**3.7.1 Incident Response Integration**

This policy integrates with RES-POL-001 (Incident Response):
- Classification criteria include student data impact
- Response procedures include school notification steps
- Post-incident review includes notification effectiveness

**3.7.2 Cross-Functional Team**

Student data breach response involves:
- Security (investigation, containment)
- Legal/Compliance (legal assessment, notification review)
- Student Data Privacy Officer (privacy assessment, school communication)
- Customer Success (customer relationship)
- Communications (if public notification needed)
- Executive leadership (significant incidents)

**3.7.3 External Resources**

As needed:
- Outside legal counsel (privacy law expertise)
- Forensic investigators
- Notification services provider
- Public relations (for significant incidents)

### 3.8 Post-Breach Activities

**3.8.1 Remediation**

Following a breach:
- Complete root cause analysis
- Implement corrective actions
- Update security controls as needed
- Document lessons learned

**3.8.2 Follow-Up with Schools**

- Provide final report to affected schools
- Confirm remediation actions
- Offer additional support as needed
- Schedule post-incident review if requested

**3.8.3 Regulatory Follow-Up**

If regulators are involved:
- Respond to inquiries promptly
- Provide requested documentation
- Cooperate with investigations
- Implement required corrective actions

**3.8.4 Documentation Retention**

Retain breach records for:
- **[Number, e.g., 7]** years (or per legal requirements)
- Duration of any litigation plus **[Number, e.g., 2]** years
- As required by DPA or regulation

### 3.9 Testing and Preparedness

**3.9.1 Notification Drills**

- Annual tabletop exercise including school notification scenario
- Test notification procedures and contacts
- Evaluate and improve process based on drills

**3.9.2 Contact Maintenance**

- Maintain current contact information for school security/privacy contacts
- Verify contacts annually or at contract renewal
- Update contacts when school notifies us of changes

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Breach** | Unauthorized access, acquisition, or disclosure of student data |
