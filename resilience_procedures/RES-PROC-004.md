# School Breach Notification Procedure (RES-PROC-004)

## 1. Purpose

This procedure provides step-by-step instructions for notifying schools, regulators, and other parties when a data breach affects student data, ensuring compliance with RES-POL-003 and applicable laws.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to Security, Legal, Privacy, Customer Success, and Communications teams involved in breach response.

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Breach Identification

#### 3.1.1 Identifying Student Data Breach

**When:** Security incident may involve student data.

**Steps:**

1. **Assess incident scope**
   - What systems are affected?
   - Does system contain student data?
   - Is student data potentially compromised?

2. **Classify as potential student data breach if:**
   - Unauthorized access to student data system
   - Unauthorized acquisition of student data
   - Unauthorized disclosure of student data
   - Loss of device/media with student data
   - Ransomware affecting student data systems

3. **Notify Student Data Privacy Officer**
   - Immediately upon identification
   - Brief description of incident

4. **Escalate per RES-POL-001**
   - Follow incident response procedures
   - Activate breach response team

### 3.2 Initial Assessment

#### 3.2.1 Assessing Breach Scope

**When:** Potential student data breach identified.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify affected schools**
   - Which schools' data may be affected?
   - Number of students potentially affected

2. **Identify data types**
   - Education records?
   - Personal information?
   - Directory information only?

3. **Assess severity**
   | Factor | High Severity | Lower Severity |
   |--------|---------------|----------------|
   | Data type | SSN, financial, health | Directory info only |
   | Volume | Large number of students | Few students |
   | Access type | Data exfiltrated | Unauthorized view only |
   | Encryption | Unencrypted | Encrypted |

4. **Determine notification obligations**
   - Which states' laws apply?
   - What are contractual requirements?
   - Timeline requirements?

5. **Document assessment**
   - Findings
   - Notification determination

### 3.3 School Notification

#### 3.3.1 Preparing Initial Notification

**When:** Breach confirmed or suspected to involve student data.

**Steps:**

1. **Draft notification content**
   - Date/time breach discovered
   - Nature of incident
   - Types of data potentially affected
   - Number of students potentially affected
   - Status of investigation
   - Immediate steps being taken
   - Point of contact

2. **Review notification**
   - Legal reviews for accuracy
   - Privacy Officer reviews for completeness
   - Executive approval for significant breaches

3. **Prepare for questions**
   - FAQ document
   - Talking points
   - Contact for inquiries

#### 3.3.2 Sending Initial Notification

**When:** Notification approved, within policy timeline.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify recipient**
   - DPA-designated security/privacy contact
   - If none designated, superintendent or IT director

2. **Send notification**
   - Email to designated contact
   - Phone call for urgent/significant breaches
   - Track delivery confirmation

3. **Document notification**
   - Content sent
   - Recipient
   - Method and timestamp
   - Delivery confirmation

4. **Timeline: Within **[Number, e.g., 24]** hours of discovery**

#### 3.3.3 Sending Updated Notification

**When:** Additional information available.

**Steps:**

1. **Draft update**
   - New findings
   - Refined scope
   - Root cause (if determined)
   - Remediation progress

2. **Review and approve**

3. **Send to same recipient**
   - Reference initial notification
   - Provide update

4. **Document update**

5. **Timeline: Within **[Number, e.g., 48]** hours or as significant updates occur**

#### 3.3.4 Sending Final Report

**When:** Investigation complete.

**Steps:**

1. **Prepare final report**
   - Summary of incident
   - Timeline of events
   - Root cause analysis
   - Final scope (students, data types affected)
   - Remediation completed
   - Steps to prevent recurrence
   - Recommendations for schools

2. **Review and approve**
   - Legal review
   - Executive review

3. **Send to schools**
   - All affected schools
   - Secure delivery

4. **Document delivery**

5. **Timeline: Within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days of discovery**

### 3.4 Regulatory Notification

#### 3.4.1 State Attorney General Notification

**When:** Required by state law.

**Steps:**

1. **Determine AG notification requirement**
   | State | Threshold | Timeline |
   |-------|-----------|----------|
   | California | >500 CA residents | Most expedient time |
   | New York | Per Education Law 2-d | Per regulation |
   | Colorado | Per state law | 30 days |
   | Connecticut | Per state law | 60 days |

2. **Prepare AG notification**
   - State-specific format
   - Required content elements

3. **Submit notification**
   - State AG portal or email
   - Track submission

4. **Document submission**
   - Date submitted
   - Content
   - Confirmation

#### 3.4.2 NY Chief Privacy Officer Notification

**When:** Breach affects New York students.

**Steps:**

1. **Prepare NY CPO notification**
   - Per 8 NYCRR 121.9 requirements
   - Include required elements

2. **Submit notification**
   - To NY State Education Department Chief Privacy Officer

3. **Document submission**

#### 3.4.3 Other Regulatory Notifications

**When:** Required by DPA, law, or regulation.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify additional requirements**
   - FTC (for COPPA violations)
   - State education departments
   - Federal agencies (if federal data)

2. **Prepare and submit notifications**
   - Per specific requirements

3. **Document all submissions**

### 3.5 Supporting Schools

#### 3.5.1 Enabling School Parent Notification

**When:** Schools need to notify parents.

**Steps:**

1. **Provide information for school notification**
   - Data to populate notification template
   - Timeline of events
   - Description of incident
   - Steps being taken

2. **Offer support**
   - Template language (if requested)
   - Substitute notification services (if schools choose)
   - Technical assistance

3. **Coordinate timing**
   - Schools may want to coordinate
   - Support school timeline

4. **Document support provided**

#### 3.5.2 Direct Parent Communication (if authorized)

**When:** School authorizes **[Company Name]** to notify parents directly.

**Steps:**

1. **Obtain written authorization**
   - School explicitly authorizes
   - Messaging approved by school

2. **Prepare parent notification**
   - School-approved content
   - Clear, understandable language
   - Action steps for parents

3. **Send notification**
   - Email to parent addresses
   - Other methods if directed

4. **Provide school visibility**
   - Copy school on communications
   - Report on delivery

5. **Document communications**
   - Content sent
   - Recipients
   - Delivery stats

### 3.6 Post-Notification Activities

#### 3.6.1 Follow-Up with Schools

**When:** After initial notification period.

**Steps:**

1. **Provide status updates**
   - Investigation progress
   - Remediation status

2. **Answer questions**
   - Respond to school inquiries
   - Provide additional information

3. **Conduct debrief** (if requested)
   - Review incident with school
   - Discuss lessons learned

4. **Document follow-up**

#### 3.6.2 Regulatory Follow-Up

**When:** Regulators have questions or require action.

**Steps:**

1. **Respond to inquiries**
   - Timely responses
   - Complete information

2. **Provide documentation**
   - As requested by regulator

3. **Implement required actions**
   - If regulator directs corrective action

4. **Document all interactions**

### 3.7 Documentation and Retention

#### 3.7.1 Maintaining Breach Records

**When:** Throughout and after breach response.

**Steps:**

1. **Document all notifications**
   - Recipients, content, dates
   - Delivery confirmations
   - Follow-up communications

2. **Document investigation**
   - Timeline, findings, evidence

3. **Document remediation**
   - Actions taken
   - Completion dates

4. **Retain records**
   - **[Number, e.g., 7]** years minimum
   - Longer if litigation

## 4. Escalation

Escalate to executive leadership when:
- Large-scale breach (>1000 students)
- Highly sensitive data (SSN, health)
- Media attention anticipated
- Regulatory inquiry received
- Legal action threatened
