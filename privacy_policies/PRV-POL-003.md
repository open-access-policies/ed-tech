# State Student Privacy Laws Policy (PRV-POL-003)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s framework for compliance with state student privacy laws that supplement federal requirements under FERPA and COPPA. As states increasingly enact their own student privacy protections, **[Company Name]** maintains a compliance program addressing the key requirements across all states where we provide services.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All student data received from K-12 schools and districts in states with student privacy laws
- All student data received from higher education institutions in states with applicable laws
- All workforce members who handle student data from schools in regulated states
- All products and services used by educational institutions

**Geographic Scope**: This policy addresses requirements in all U.S. states where **[Company Name]** provides services to educational institutions. Requirements vary by state; see ANNEX-003 (State Privacy Law Matrix) for state-specific details.

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** complies with state student privacy laws as an integral part of our commitment to protecting student data. Our baseline practices meet or exceed the requirements of the most protective state laws.

### 3.1 Key State Laws

**3.1.1 California - SOPIPA (Student Online Personal Information Protection Act)**

**Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §§ 22584-22585**

SOPIPA applies to operators of websites, online services, or apps designed and marketed for K-12 school purposes.

| Requirement | [Company Name] Compliance |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| **No targeted advertising** based on student information | Prohibited per STU-POL-001 Section 3.2.2 |
| **No profile building** for non-educational purposes | Prohibited per STU-POL-001 Section 3.2.2 |
| **No sale of student information** | Prohibited per STU-POL-004 |
| **No disclosure** except for K-12 purposes | Limited to contracted educational purposes |
| **Security procedures** appropriate to data sensitivity | Per SEC-POL-001 |
| **Delete upon request** from school/district | Per STU-POL-005 |
| **Disclose data practices** in privacy policy | Privacy policy includes required disclosures |

**3.1.2 New York - Education Law 2-d**

**NY Educ. Law § 2-d; 8 NYCRR Part 121**

NY Education Law 2-d applies to third-party contractors that receive student data or teacher/principal data from educational agencies.

| Requirement | [Company Name] Compliance |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Parents' Bill of Rights** acknowledgment | Provided to all NY schools |
| **Data security and privacy plan** | Maintained per this policy |
| **Encryption** (data in motion) | TLS 1.2+ per SEC-POL-001 |
| **Encryption** (data at rest for mobile devices) | AES-256 encryption |
| **Data breach notification** within specified timeframe | Within **[Number, e.g., 24]** hours per RES-POL-003 |
| **Annual third-party contractor certification** | Provided upon request |
| **Subcontractor limitations** | Per STU-POL-004 |

## 5. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **SOPIPA** | Student Online Personal Information Protection Act (California) |
| **Education Law 2-d** | New York Education Law Section 2-d and implementing regulations |
| **Student Data** | As defined under applicable state law; generally, personally identifiable information contained in education records |
| **Operator** | Under SOPIPA, an operator of a website, online service, or app with actual knowledge that the service is used primarily for K-12 school purposes |
| **Covered Information** | Under SOPIPA, personally identifiable information or material created or provided by a student, parent, or school employee |
| **Third-Party Contractor** | Under NY 2-d, any person or entity that receives student data or teacher/principal data from an educational agency |
