# COPPA Compliance Policy (PRV-POL-002)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s compliance framework for the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), 15 U.S.C. §§ 6501-6506, and its implementing regulations at 16 CFR Part 312. This policy ensures that **[Company Name]** protects the privacy of children under 13 when providing educational technology services to K-12 schools.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All products and services directed to children under 13
- All products and services used in K-12 educational settings
- All personal information collected from children under 13
- All workforce members, contractors, and third parties who access children's personal information

**Applicability Note:**
- **K-12 Products**: Presumed to be used by children under 13; COPPA applies
- **Higher Education Products**: COPPA generally does not apply (students 18+), but may apply to dual-enrollment programs
- **Mixed-Age Products**: Must have mechanisms to identify and protect children under 13

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** is committed to protecting the privacy of children under 13 and complying with COPPA requirements when providing educational technology services.

### 3.1 COPPA Applicability

**3.1.1 When COPPA Applies**

COPPA applies when **[Company Name]**:
- Operates a website or online service **directed to children** under 13; OR
- Has **actual knowledge** that it is collecting personal information from a child under 13

**3.1.2 "Directed to Children" Determination**

Factors that indicate a service is directed to children:
- Subject matter appeals to children (educational games, children's content)
- Visual or audio content designed for children
- Use of animated characters or child-oriented activities
- Child's age as target audience
- Presence of advertising for products directed to children
- Marketing to K-12 schools

**[Company Name]** treats all K-12 educational products as potentially directed to children and applies COPPA protections accordingly.

### 3.2 Consent Requirements

**3.2.1 School Consent Model**

When providing educational services contracted by schools, **[Company Name]** relies on schools to provide consent on behalf of parents under the "school consent" model permitted by the FTC.

**School Consent Criteria:**

Schools may consent on behalf of parents when:
- The service is used solely for an **educational purpose**
- The school **benefits from the collection** for educational purposes
- Personal information is **not used for commercial purposes** unrelated to the educational context

**[Company Name]** obtains school consent through:
- Signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Terms of Service acknowledgment by authorized school representative
- Written agreement specifying permitted uses

**3.2.2 Direct Parental Consent**

When school consent is not available or appropriate, **[Company Name]** obtains verifiable parental consent using FTC-approved methods:

| Consent Method | Description | Use Case |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Signed consent form** | Physical form returned by mail, fax, or electronic scan | Initial enrollment |
| **Credit card transaction** | In connection with a monetary transaction | Paid home-use subscriptions |
| **Video conference** | Video call with parent and trained personnel | Complex consent situations |
| **Government ID verification** | Check parent ID against government database | High-risk data collection |
| **Knowledge-based authentication** | Challenge questions from third-party database | Online consent process |
| **Email plus** | Email notification with delayed activity and additional confirmation mechanism | Low-risk internal operations |

**3.2.3 Consent Scope and Limitations**

Consent is specific to:
- The types of personal information collected
- The uses of personal information
- Disclosure to third parties (if any)

Consent must be renewed if **[Company Name]** materially changes its data practices.

### 3.3 Notice Requirements

**3.3.1 Notice to Schools**

Before collecting personal information from children through school-contracted services, **[Company Name]** provides schools with:

- Types of personal information collected
- How personal information is used
- Disclosure practices (including subcontractors)
- Privacy policy URL
- Contact information for the Student Data Privacy Officer

**3.3.2 Direct Notice to Parents**

When collecting personal information directly (not through schools), **[Company Name]** provides parents:

- **Direct Notice**: Description of information collected and how it will be used
- **Privacy Policy Link**: Easily accessible from any page where information is collected
- **Contact Information**: How to reach the operator about privacy concerns

**3.3.3 Privacy Policy Requirements**

**[Company Name]**'s privacy policy includes:

- Name and contact information (including email) for all operators collecting personal information
- Types of personal information collected from children
- How personal information is collected (directly or passively)
- How personal information is used
- Whether personal information is disclosed to third parties and for what purposes
- That parents can review their child's information, request deletion, and refuse further collection
- Procedures for parents to exercise their rights

### 3.4 Data Collection Limitations

**3.4.1 Collection Minimization**

**[Company Name]** collects only personal information **reasonably necessary** to:
- Provide the educational service
- Ensure the security of the service
- Fulfill a specific request from the child (with school consent)

**Prohibited Collection:**
- Personal information not reasonably necessary for the activity
- Information collected as a condition of participation (beyond what is reasonably necessary)
- Persistent identifiers for targeted advertising

**3.4.2 Personal Information Definition**

Under COPPA, personal information from children includes:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Direct Identifiers** | First and last name, home address, email address, telephone number, Social Security number |
| **Online Identifiers** | Screen name, username, persistent identifiers (cookies, IP addresses when used to contact a specific individual) |
| **Biometric/Media** | Photograph, video, audio file with child's image or voice |
| **Location** | Geolocation information sufficient to identify street name and city |
| **Combined Information** | Any other information combined with the above that would permit physical or online contact |

### 3.5 Use and Disclosure Restrictions

**3.5.1 Permitted Uses**

Personal information from children may only be used for:
- Providing the contracted educational service
- Internal operations (security, maintaining or analyzing service function, serving contextual ads, frequency capping)
- Responding to child/school requests
- Protecting safety of the child or service
- As required by law

**3.5.2 Prohibited Uses**

Personal information from children shall NOT be used for:
- **Behavioral advertising**: Targeting ads based on child's personal information or online behavior
- **Profile building**: Creating profiles about children for commercial purposes
- **Sale or rental**: Selling personal information to third parties
- **Conditioning participation**: Requiring more information than necessary as a condition of activity

**3.5.3 Disclosure to Third Parties**

Personal information from children may only be disclosed:
- To service providers performing essential functions (under contract with COPPA compliance requirements)
- To protect child safety or site security
- As required by law or legal process
- If the school has authorized the disclosure

### 3.6 Parental Rights

**3.6.1 Right to Review**

Parents (or schools on behalf of parents) may request to review personal information collected from their child. **[Company Name]**:
- Verifies the identity of the requestor
- Provides information within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days
- Provides information in a readable format

**3.6.2 Right to Deletion**

Parents (or schools) may request deletion of their child's personal information. **[Company Name]**:
- Deletes information within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days (unless retention is legally required)
- Confirms deletion to the requestor
- May need to terminate the child's participation if information is essential to the service

**3.6.3 Right to Refuse Further Collection**

Parents (or schools) may refuse to permit further collection or use of personal information. **[Company Name]**:
- Stops further collection within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days
- Informs the parent/school of any consequences (e.g., service termination)
- Does not condition service on waiver of this right

### 3.7 Data Retention and Security

**3.7.1 Retention Limitation**

Personal information from children is retained only as long as reasonably necessary to:
- Fulfill the purpose for which it was collected
- Meet legal or business record-keeping requirements

When personal information is no longer needed, it is deleted using secure methods.

**3.7.2 Security Requirements**

**[Company Name]** maintains reasonable security measures to protect children's personal information from:
- Unauthorized access
- Disclosure
- Alteration
- Destruction

See SEC-POL-001 (Information Security Policy) for detailed security controls.

### 3.8 Third-Party Service Providers

**3.8.1 Due Diligence**

Before engaging service providers who access children's personal information:
- Evaluate their privacy practices and security measures
- Verify they can comply with COPPA requirements

**3.8.2 Contractual Requirements**

Service provider agreements include:
- Restrictions on use of personal information
- Security requirements
- Confidentiality obligations
- Notification requirements for security incidents
- Audit rights

**3.8.3 Ongoing Monitoring**

**[Company Name]** takes reasonable steps to ensure service providers maintain COPPA compliance, including:
- Periodic assessments of privacy practices
- Review of security controls
- Immediate action if non-compliance is discovered

## 5. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Child** | An individual under the age of 13 (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **Operator** | Any person who operates a website or online service and collects personal information from users, or on whose behalf such information is collected (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **Parent** | Includes legal guardians (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **Personal Information** | Individually identifiable information about a child collected online, as defined in 16 CFR 312.2 |
| **Directed to Children** | A website or online service, or portion thereof, targeted to children (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent** | Any reasonable effort to ensure that a parent authorizes collection, use, and/or disclosure of personal information (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **School Consent** | Consent provided by schools on behalf of parents for educational purposes, as permitted by FTC guidance |
