# School Official Designation Policy (STU-POL-002)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes the criteria and requirements for **[Company Name]** to operate as a "school official" under FERPA (34 CFR 99.31(a)(1)). As a school official, **[Company Name]** may receive education records from educational institutions without requiring individual consent from parents or eligible students, provided specific conditions are met.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All contracts and agreements with educational institutions (K-12 and higher education)
- All education records received from schools under the school official exception
- All workforce members who access education records
- All products and services provided to educational institutions

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** maintains school official status by meeting all FERPA requirements and operating under the direct control of contracting educational institutions.

### 3.1 Criteria for School Official Status

To qualify as a school official under FERPA, **[Company Name]** ensures all four criteria are satisfied:

| Criterion | FERPA Requirement | How [Company Name] Satisfies |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Institutional Function** | Performs an institutional service or function for which the school would otherwise use employees | DPA specifies educational services provided |
| **Direct Control** | Under direct control of the school regarding use and maintenance of education records | DPA establishes oversight rights; use limitations |
| **Use Limitation** | Uses education records only for authorized purposes specified in the contract | DPA restricts to specified educational purposes |
| **Annual Notification** | Meets criteria specified in school's annual FERPA notification to parents | Provides information for school notifications |

### 3.2 Data Processing Agreement Requirements

Every agreement with an educational institution includes:

**3.2.1 Service Description**
- Clear description of educational services provided
- Types of education records required to provide services
- How records will be used to support educational purposes

**3.2.2 Direct Control Provisions**
- School retains authority over data use and handling
- School may audit **[Company Name]** compliance
- School directs data retention and deletion
- School may terminate access upon contract end

**3.2.3 Use Restrictions**
- Records used only for purposes specified in the agreement
- No secondary uses without school authorization
- No commercial use unrelated to contracted services
- No sharing with unauthorized third parties

**3.2.4 Compliance Commitments**
- Compliance with FERPA, COPPA, and applicable state laws
- Security measures appropriate to data sensitivity
- Incident notification procedures
- Employee training requirements

### 3.3 Maintaining Direct Control

**3.3.1 School Authority**

Schools maintain control over education records through:

| Control Mechanism | [Company Name] Implementation |
|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Data access decisions** | Schools control which students/staff access the service |
| **Feature configuration** | Schools configure data collection and sharing settings |
| **Audit rights** | Schools may audit our use of their data |
| **Termination rights** | Schools may terminate and reclaim data at any time |
| **Amendment authority** | Schools direct corrections to education records |

**3.3.2 Use Limitations**

**[Company Name]** demonstrates direct control by:
- Using data only for specified educational purposes
- Not using data for **[Company Name]**'s independent purposes
- Not creating derivative works from education records for non-educational use
- Not commingling school data for commercial benefit

### 3.4 Annual Notification Support

**3.4.1 Information for Schools**

**[Company Name]** provides schools with information for their annual FERPA notification:
- Company name and contact information
- Description of services provided
- Types of education records accessed
- Confirmation of school official status
- Statement of compliance with FERPA requirements

**3.4.2 Template Language**

**[Company Name]** provides schools with suggested notification language:

> "[Company Name] is designated as a school official for purposes of providing [description of services]. [Company Name] receives [types of data] to provide these services and is prohibited from using education records for purposes other than those specified in the school's agreement with [Company Name]."

### 3.5 Contract Termination and Data Return

**3.5.1 Upon Contract Termination**

When a school's contract ends:
1. **Access termination**: All access to school's education records ends
2. **Data export**: School may request data export in standard format
3. **Data deletion**: Remaining data deleted within **[Number, e.g., 60]** days
4. **Certification**: Destruction certificate provided upon request

**3.5.2 Survival of Obligations**

Even after termination:
- Confidentiality obligations continue
- No retained copies may be used
- Audit rights survive for **[Number, e.g., 2]** years for compliance verification

### 3.6 Subcontractor Requirements

**3.6.1 Flow-Down Obligations**

When **[Company Name]** engages subcontractors who access education records:
- Subcontractors are bound by equivalent FERPA obligations
- Written agreements require compliance with this policy
- Schools are notified of material subcontractors
- **[Company Name]** remains responsible for subcontractor compliance

**3.6.2 Approved Subcontractors**

**[Company Name]** maintains a list of approved subcontractors who may access education records. Schools may request this list and may object to specific subcontractors per DPA terms.

### 3.7 Documentation and Verification

**3.7.1 Contract Documentation**

**[Company Name]** maintains:
- Executed DPA for each contracting school/district
- Records of school authorizations and configurations
- Documentation of school requests and our responses
- Evidence of compliance with school directions

**3.7.2 Compliance Verification**

Schools may verify **[Company Name]**'s school official status through:
- Annual compliance attestations
- Security questionnaire responses
- On-request audit reports
- Third-party certifications (SOC 2)

## 5. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **School Official** | Under FERPA, a contractor or service provider that meets the criteria in 34 CFR 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B) |
| **Direct Control** | The school's ability to direct how education records are used and maintained |
| **Legitimate Educational Interest** | The interest a school official has when they need to review education records to fulfill their professional responsibility |
| **Education Records** | Records directly related to a student maintained by an educational agency or institution or a party acting for it |
