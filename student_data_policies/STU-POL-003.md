# Parental Consent and Rights Policy (STU-POL-003)

## 1. Objective

This policy establishes **[Company Name]**'s framework for managing parental consent and honoring the rights of parents and eligible students under FERPA, COPPA, and applicable state laws. This policy ensures parents can exercise their rights to access, review, correct, and delete their children's education records and personal information.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All student data and education records maintained by **[Company Name]**
- All requests from parents, guardians, and eligible students
- All workforce members who process rights requests
- Both K-12 and higher education contexts

**Rights Holder by Context:**
- **K-12 (under 18)**: Parents exercise rights; some state laws provide students limited rights
- **Higher Education / Age 18+**: "Eligible students" exercise rights directly
- **Dual Enrollment**: Rights may transfer to the student if attending postsecondary institution

## 3. Policy

**[Company Name]** supports parents and eligible students in exercising their privacy rights and facilitates schools in meeting their FERPA and COPPA obligations.

### 3.1 Consent Framework

**3.1.1 School-Based Consent (Primary Model)**

For educational services provided through school contracts:
- Schools provide consent for data processing on behalf of parents
- Schools determine data to be shared with **[Company Name]**
- **[Company Name]** acts under school's authority per the Data Processing Agreement

**When School Consent Applies:**
- Services contracted by the school/district
- Data used solely for educational purposes
- No commercial use beyond educational context

**3.1.2 Direct Parental Consent**

When school consent is insufficient or unavailable:

| Scenario | Consent Approach |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Home-use product purchased by parent** | Direct verifiable parental consent required |
| **Features beyond school contract** | School must authorize or parent must consent |
| **Children under 13 (COPPA)** | Verifiable consent per PRV-POL-002 |
| **Marketing communications** | Opt-in consent from parent |

**Verifiable Consent Methods:**
- Signed consent form (mail, fax, scan)
- Credit card transaction
- Video verification
- Knowledge-based authentication
- Email-plus (for internal operations only)

### 3.2 Right to Access and Review

**3.2.1 Request Process**

Parents and eligible students have the right to review education records. Requests should be submitted to the school, which forwards to **[Company Name]** when records are in our custody.

**Process:**
1. School receives and verifies request
2. School forwards request to **[Company Name]**
3. **[Company Name]** compiles responsive records within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days
4. School provides records to parent/eligible student within 45 days (FERPA requirement)

**3.2.2 Information Provided**

Upon valid request, **[Company Name]** provides:
- All education records directly related to the student
- Records in an accessible, understandable format
- Explanation of data elements if requested

**[Company Name]** does not charge for providing access to records.

### 3.3 Right to Request Amendment

**3.3.1 Amendment Requests**

Parents and eligible students may request correction of education records they believe are inaccurate, misleading, or violate privacy rights.

**Process:**
1. Request submitted to school
2. School forwards to **[Company Name]** with direction
3. **[Company Name]** corrects records as directed by school
4. Correction confirmed within **[Number, e.g., 10]** business days

**3.3.2 Scope of Amendments**

**[Company Name]** corrects:
- Factual errors in data fields
- Data attributed to the wrong student
- Information the school directs us to correct

**Note:** The right to amendment does not extend to grades or other evaluative content; schools retain authority over such determinations.

### 3.4 Right to Deletion

**3.4.1 Deletion Requests**

Parents (or eligible students) may request deletion of their child's personal information through the school.

**FERPA Context:**
- Schools direct deletion; **[Company Name]** follows school instruction
- Schools maintain records per state retention requirements

**COPPA Context:**
- Parents may request deletion of child's (under 13) personal information
- **[Company Name]** deletes within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days unless legally required to retain

**State Law Context:**
- Many states provide deletion rights (see PRV-POL-003)
- **[Company Name]** honors requests per applicable state law

**3.4.2 Deletion Process**

1. School receives and verifies request
2. School directs **[Company Name]** to delete specific data
3. **[Company Name]** deletes data from all systems within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days
4. **[Company Name]** confirms deletion to school
5. Child's access to service may be terminated if data is essential

**3.4.3 Exceptions to Deletion**

Data may be retained despite deletion request when:
- Required by law or legal process
- Necessary to complete a transaction requested by the student
- Required for contractual obligations with the school
- In aggregated/de-identified form (not personal information)

### 3.5 Right to Refuse Further Collection

**3.5.1 Opt-Out Rights**

Parents may refuse to permit further collection of their child's personal information.

**[Company Name]** Response:**
- Ceases further collection within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days
- Informs school and parent of any service limitations
- Does not condition essential services on waiving this right

**3.5.2 Impact on Services**

If data collection is essential to the service:
- Parent is informed of consequences (e.g., service termination)
- School determines whether continued participation is appropriate
- Alternative arrangements may be available

### 3.6 Consent Withdrawal

**3.6.1 Withdrawing Consent**

Parents who previously consented to data collection may withdraw consent.

**Process:**
1. Parent notifies school (or **[Company Name]** for direct relationships)
2. **[Company Name]** stops further collection/processing within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days
3. Previously collected data deleted per Section 3.4 (unless retention is legally required)
4. Service access terminated if data is essential

**3.6.2 Effect of Withdrawal**

Consent withdrawal:
- Applies prospectively (does not retroactively invalidate prior use)
- May result in service termination
- Does not affect lawfully processed data prior to withdrawal

### 3.8 Verification Requirements

Before processing rights requests, **[Company Name]** verifies:

| Requestor | Verification Method |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **School** | Authorized contact per DPA |
| **Parent (through school)** | School verifies; **[Company Name]** relies on school |

## 5. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Parent** | Natural parent, guardian, or individual acting as a parent (34 CFR 99.3) |
| **Eligible Student** | Student who has reached 18 or attends postsecondary institution (34 CFR 99.3) |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent** | Reasonable effort to ensure parent authorizes collection/use (16 CFR 312.2) |
| **Education Records** | Records directly related to student maintained by educational agency or acting party |
