# School Official Designation Procedure (STU-PROC-002)

## 1. Purpose

This procedure provides step-by-step instructions for establishing and maintaining school official status under FERPA, including DPA execution, direct control verification, and annual notification support.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to Sales, Legal, Customer Success, and Implementation teams who manage school relationships and agreements.

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Establishing School Official Status

#### 3.1.1 DPA Negotiation and Execution

**When:** New school or district customer is onboarded.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify DPA approach**
   - Check if customer has their own DPA → offer to review and sign
   - Check if state standard DPA applies (SDPC, state template) → use standard
   - If neither → propose **[Company Name]** standard DPA

2. **Review customer DPA (if provided)**
   - Legal reviews for acceptable terms
   - Identify any redlines needed
   - Negotiate mutually acceptable terms
   - Escalate to General Counsel if impasse

3. **Prepare DPA for execution**
   - Complete all variable fields (school name, services, data elements)
   - Include state-specific addenda if required
   - Route for internal approval (Legal + Privacy Officer)

4. **Execute DPA**
   - Obtain authorized signature from school representative
   - Countersign on behalf of **[Company Name]**
   - Distribute fully executed copy to both parties

5. **File DPA**
   - Upload to contract management system
   - Tag with school, effective date, expiration
   - Set reminder for renewal review

#### 3.1.2 Verifying School Official Criteria

**When:** DPA is executed; annually during contract.

**Steps:**

1. **Institutional function criterion**
   - Confirm DPA specifies educational services
   - Verify services are ones school would otherwise provide
   - Document in customer record

2. **Direct control criterion**
   - Confirm DPA grants school control over data use
   - Confirm school can audit compliance
   - Confirm school directs retention and deletion
   - Document control mechanisms in place

3. **Use limitation criterion**
   - Confirm DPA restricts use to specified purposes
   - Verify no commercial use beyond educational context
   - Document permitted uses

4. **Annual notification criterion**
   - Provide school with notification information (see Section 3.2)
   - Confirm school includes **[Company Name]** in annual FERPA notice

5. **Document verification**
   - Complete school official verification checklist
   - Store in customer record
   - Note any concerns for follow-up

### 3.2 Annual FERPA Notification Support

#### 3.2.1 Preparing Notification Information

**When:** Annually, before school's FERPA notification deadline (typically start of school year).

**Steps:**

1. **Generate notification package**
   - Company name and contact information
   - Description of services provided to the school
   - Types of education records accessed
   - Statement of school official status
   - FERPA compliance commitment

2. **Customize for school**
   - Include school-specific services if applicable
   - Reference specific DPA if helpful

3. **Send to school**
   - Email to designated school contact
   - Request confirmation of receipt
   - Offer assistance with notification language

#### 3.2.2 Providing Template Language

**When:** School requests sample notification language.

**Steps:**

1. **Provide template**
   ```
   "[Company Name] is designated as a school official for purposes of
   providing [description of services]. [Company Name] receives
   [types of data] to provide these services and is prohibited from
   using education records for purposes other than those specified
   in the school's agreement with [Company Name]."
   ```

2. **Customize template**
   - Insert school-specific service description
   - Insert data types per DPA

3. **Review school's draft** (if requested)
   - Confirm accuracy
   - Suggest corrections if needed

### 3.3 Maintaining Direct Control

#### 3.3.1 Processing School Directions

**When:** School provides direction regarding their data.

**Steps:**

1. **Receive direction**
   - Document the direction received
   - Verify from authorized school contact
   - Confirm understanding with school

2. **Evaluate feasibility**
   - Can direction be implemented?
   - Any technical or legal constraints?

3. **Implement direction**
   - Execute school's direction
   - Document actions taken
   - Confirm completion to school

4. **Record for audit**
   - Log direction and response in customer record
   - Retain for audit and compliance purposes

#### 3.3.2 Facilitating School Audits

**When:** School exercises audit rights under DPA.

**Steps:**

1. **Receive audit request**
   - Document scope and timing
   - Identify school contact for audit

2. **Coordinate internally**
   - Identify responsible teams
   - Gather requested documentation
   - Schedule any on-site or virtual sessions

3. **Provide audit support**
   - Share requested documentation
   - Answer auditor questions
   - Provide system access (read-only) if requested

4. **Address findings**
   - Review any audit findings
   - Develop remediation plan if needed
   - Report remediation to school

5. **Document audit**
   - Retain audit correspondence
   - File any reports or findings
   - Track remediation to completion

### 3.4 Contract Termination

#### 3.4.1 Managing Contract End

**When:** School contract ends (non-renewal, termination, or school closure).

**Steps:**

1. **Notify school of upcoming termination**
   - Send notice per DPA timeline (e.g., 30 days prior)
   - Offer data export options
   - Confirm school's data disposition preference

2. **Process data export (if requested)**
   - Generate data export in agreed format
   - Provide secure transfer to school
   - Confirm receipt by school

3. **Terminate access**
   - Disable school administrator accounts
   - Disable user accounts for school
   - Remove integration connections

4. **Delete data**
   - Initiate data deletion per STU-POL-005
   - Complete within DPA timeline (e.g., 60 days)
   - Retain only data legally required

5. **Provide destruction certificate**
   - Generate certificate of destruction
   - Send to school contact
   - Retain copy in customer record

6. **Close customer record**
   - Document termination date and reason
   - Archive customer record per retention schedule
