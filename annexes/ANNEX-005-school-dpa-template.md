This template provides a standard Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for use with K-12 schools and districts. It establishes **[Company Name]** as a school official under FERPA and addresses requirements of state student privacy laws.

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, School desires to use Provider's educational technology services for the benefit of its students and staff;

WHEREAS, Provider will receive and process Student Data in connection with providing these services;

WHEREAS, School and Provider desire to ensure the protection of Student Data in compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act ("FERPA"), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act ("COPPA"), applicable state student privacy laws, and this DPA;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Student Data"** means personally identifiable information from education records as defined in FERPA (34 CFR § 99.3), and personal information from or about students as defined in COPPA (16 CFR § 312.2), that is provided to or collected by Provider in connection with the Services.

**1.2 "Education Records"** means records directly related to a student maintained by an educational agency or institution or by a party acting for such agency or institution, as defined in FERPA (34 CFR § 99.3).

**1.3 "Services"** means the educational technology products and services provided by Provider to School as described in Exhibit A.

**1.4 "School Official"** means a contractor that meets the criteria in 34 CFR § 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B) and is authorized to access education records without parent consent.

**1.5 "Legitimate Educational Interest"** means the school official's need to review education records to fulfill their professional responsibility for the School.

---

## ARTICLE 2: SCHOOL OFFICIAL DESIGNATION

**2.1 Designation.** School designates Provider as a School Official for the purpose of providing the Services. Provider performs an institutional service or function for which School would otherwise use its own employees.

**2.2 Direct Control.** Provider operates under the direct control of School with respect to the use and maintenance of Student Data. School retains authority over data use and may audit Provider's compliance.

**2.3 Use Limitation.** Provider uses Student Data only for the purposes specified in this DPA and as directed by School. Provider does not use Student Data for any purpose not authorized by School or this DPA.

**2.4 Annual Notification.** Provider's receipt of Student Data is consistent with School's annual FERPA notification to parents. Provider provides School with information necessary for School's annual notification.

---

## ARTICLE 3: SERVICES AND DATA

**3.1 Services.** Provider provides the Services described in Exhibit A to School.

**3.2 Student Data Elements.** The categories of Student Data to be processed are listed in Exhibit B.

**3.3 Purpose.** Provider processes Student Data solely to provide the Services and for no other purpose.

**3.4 Data Minimization.** Provider collects only Student Data necessary to provide the Services. Provider does not request or require provision of Student Data beyond what is necessary.

---

## ARTICLE 4: PROHIBITED USES

**4.1 No Sale.** Provider shall NOT sell Student Data to any third party. "Sell" means the exchange of Student Data for monetary or other valuable consideration.

**4.2 No Advertising.** Provider shall NOT use Student Data to target advertising to students, including behavioral advertising based on Student Data or student online activity.

**4.3 No Profiling.** Provider shall NOT use Student Data to create commercial profiles about students for purposes other than providing the Services.

**4.4 No Unauthorized Disclosure.** Provider shall NOT disclose Student Data to any third party except as authorized in this DPA.

---

## ARTICLE 5: SECURITY

**5.1 Security Program.** Provider maintains administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of Student Data, including:
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher)
- Encryption of data at rest
- Access controls and authentication
- Employee training on data privacy and security
- Incident response procedures

**5.2 Security Standards.** Provider's security program is designed to meet or exceed industry standards, including SOC 2 Type II.

**5.3 Access Controls.** Provider restricts access to Student Data to personnel with a legitimate educational interest who require access to perform their job functions.

---

## ARTICLE 6: SUBCONTRACTORS

**6.1 Subcontractor Use.** Provider may engage subcontractors to assist in providing the Services, provided that:
- Subcontractors are bound by written agreements with privacy and security obligations equivalent to this DPA
- Provider remains responsible for subcontractor compliance
- Provider provides a list of subcontractors upon School's request

**6.2 Subcontractor List.** Provider's current subcontractors with access to Student Data are listed in Exhibit C.

**6.3 Objection.** School may object to Provider's use of a new subcontractor by notifying Provider in writing within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days of receiving notice. The parties will work in good faith to address School's concerns.

---

## ARTICLE 7: DATA BREACH

**7.1 Definition.** "Data Breach" means unauthorized access, acquisition, or disclosure of Student Data, or other security incident that compromises the confidentiality of Student Data.

**7.2 Notification.** Provider notifies School of any suspected or confirmed Data Breach within **[Number, e.g., 24]** hours of discovery. Notification includes:
- Nature of the incident
- Types of Student Data potentially affected
- Number of students potentially affected
- Steps being taken to address the incident

**7.3 Cooperation.** Provider cooperates with School in investigating and responding to the Data Breach, including providing information necessary for School to comply with breach notification laws.

**7.4 Costs.** Provider bears the costs of investigating and remediating a Data Breach caused by Provider's acts or omissions.

---

## ARTICLE 8: DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

**8.1 Retention.** Provider retains Student Data only for as long as necessary to provide the Services, unless School directs otherwise.

**8.2 Deletion.** Upon termination of this DPA or School's request, Provider deletes or destroys all Student Data within **[Number, e.g., 60]** days, except as required by law.

**8.3 Return of Data.** Prior to deletion, School may request return of Student Data in a commonly used electronic format. Provider provides such data within **[Number, e.g., 30]** days of request.

**8.4 Certification.** Upon request, Provider provides written certification of data deletion.

---

## ARTICLE 9: PARENT AND STUDENT RIGHTS

**9.1 Support for Rights.** Provider supports School in responding to parent and eligible student requests to:
- Access and review Student Data
- Request amendment of inaccurate data
- Request deletion of data

**9.2 Response Timeline.** Provider responds to School-forwarded requests within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days and provides responsive information within the timeframe required by law.

**9.3 No Direct Contact.** Provider does not communicate directly with parents or students regarding their data without School's authorization.

---

## ARTICLE 10: AUDITS AND COMPLIANCE

**10.1 Audit Rights.** School may audit Provider's compliance with this DPA upon reasonable notice. Provider cooperates with such audits and provides reasonable access to relevant records.

**10.2 Compliance Verification.** Upon request, Provider provides:
- SOC 2 report or equivalent
- Responses to security questionnaires
- Evidence of employee training
- Other compliance documentation

**10.3 Annual Certification.** Provider provides annual certification of compliance with this DPA upon School's request.

---

## ARTICLE 11: TERM AND TERMINATION

**11.1 Term.** This DPA is effective as of the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

**11.2 Termination.** Either party may terminate this DPA:
- Upon **[Number, e.g., 30]** days written notice
- Immediately upon material breach that is not cured within **[Number, e.g., 15]** days of notice

**11.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:
- Provider ceases processing Student Data
- Provider returns or deletes Student Data per Article 8
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination

---

## ARTICLE 12: GENERAL PROVISIONS

**12.1 Compliance with Law.** Both parties comply with FERPA, COPPA, and applicable state student privacy laws.

**12.2 Amendments.** This DPA may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

**12.3 Entire Agreement.** This DPA, including its Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement regarding Student Data and supersedes prior agreements on this subject.

**12.4 Governing Law.** This DPA is governed by the laws of **[State]**.

**12.5 Notices.** Notices under this DPA are sent to the contacts listed above or as updated in writing.
