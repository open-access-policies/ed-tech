# FERPA Compliance Procedure (PRV-PROC-001)

## 1. Purpose

This procedure provides step-by-step instructions for ensuring FERPA compliance in **[Company Name]**'s handling of education records, including school official verification, disclosure management, and record-keeping.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all workforce members who handle education records from K-12 and higher education institutions.

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Education Records Handling

#### 3.1.1 Identifying Education Records

**When:** Receiving or processing data from educational institutions.

**Steps:**

1. **Evaluate each data element**
   - Is it directly related to a student?
   - Is it maintained by the school or on behalf of the school?

2. **Check exclusions**
   | Exclusion | Description |
   |-----------|-------------|
   | Sole possession notes | Personal notes not shared |
   | Law enforcement records | Created by law enforcement unit |
   | Employment records | Student employed by school (not as student) |
   | Alumni records | Created after student no longer attends |

3. **Classify accordingly**
   - If education record → apply FERPA protections
   - If excluded → document exclusion basis

4. **Document classification**
   - Record in data inventory
   - Note basis for classification

#### 3.1.2 Handling Directory Information

**When:** Processing data that may be directory information.

**Steps:**

1. **Verify school designation**
   - Has school designated this data as directory information?
   - Check school's annual notification or DPA

2. **Check opt-out status**
   - Has parent/student opted out of directory information?
   - Honor opt-outs in processing

3. **Apply appropriate protections**
   - Directory information has fewer restrictions
   - But still requires school authorization for disclosure
   - Document handling basis

### 3.2 School Official Verification

#### 3.2.1 Verifying School Official Status

**When:** Annually and when changes occur.

**Steps:**

1. **Review DPA terms**
   - Services described and limited
   - Direct control provisions present
   - Use restrictions documented
   - Audit rights included

2. **Verify four criteria**
   | Criterion | Verification |
   |-----------|-------------|
   | Institutional function | Services described in DPA |
   | Direct control | School control provisions in DPA |
   | Use limitation | Restrictions documented in DPA |
   | Annual notification | Information provided to school |

3. **Document verification**
   - Complete verification checklist
   - Store in customer record

4. **Address gaps**
   - If any criterion not met → work to remediate
   - May require DPA amendment

### 3.3 Permitted Disclosures

#### 3.3.1 Evaluating Disclosure Requests

**When:** Request to disclose education records outside normal operations.

**Steps:**

1. **Identify disclosure basis**
   | Basis | Requirements |
   |-------|-------------|
   | School official | Per DPA; for contracted services |
   | Consent | Written consent from parent/student |
   | School direction | Written instruction from school |
   | Legal process | Valid subpoena/court order |
   | Health/safety emergency | Document emergency circumstances |
   | Studies exception | Per 34 CFR 99.31(a)(6) |

2. **Verify requirements met**
   - School direction: from authorized contact
   - Consent: proper form, scope, signature
   - Legal: valid process, scope appropriate
   - Emergency: documented threat

3. **Limit to minimum necessary**
   - Only data required for purpose
   - Document scope limitation

4. **Execute disclosure**
   - Secure transmission
   - Document disclosure

5. **Record per FERPA**
   - Add to disclosure log (see Section 3.4)

#### 3.3.2 Disclosures Without Consent

**When:** Disclosure may be permitted without parent/student consent.

**Steps:**

1. **Evaluate exception applicability**
   - See PRV-POL-001 Section 3.3 for FERPA exceptions

2. **For legal process:**
   - Verify validity with Legal
   - Notify school (unless prohibited)
   - Limit scope to required data

3. **For emergencies:**
   - Document emergency circumstances
   - Notify school promptly after

4. **For school officials (subcontractors):**
   - Verify subcontractor agreement includes required terms
   - Limit to contracted services

5. **Document disclosure and basis**

### 3.4 FERPA Record-Keeping

#### 3.4.1 Maintaining Disclosure Records

**When:** Disclosures of education records occur.

**Steps:**

1. **Record each disclosure** (except routine school disclosures)
   - Party receiving records
   - Legitimate interest in records
   - Date of disclosure
   - Records disclosed

2. **Maintain for each student**
   - Organized by student/school
   - Accessible for audit

3. **Provide to schools on request**
   - Schools maintain official FERPA log
   - **[Company Name]** provides records to support

4. **Retain per FERPA**
   - As long as education records are maintained
   - Plus retention after destruction

### 3.5 Parent/Student Rights Support

#### 3.5.1 Supporting FERPA Rights

**When:** School forwards parent/student rights request.

**Steps:**

1. **Access requests**
   - Compile records within **[Number, e.g., 5]** business days
   - Provide to school for parent delivery
   - Help school meet 45-day FERPA timeline

2. **Amendment requests**
   - School directs amendments
   - Execute corrections as directed
   - Document amendments made

3. **Complaints**
   - If parent/student complains about FERPA compliance
   - Investigate promptly
   - Report findings to school
   - Remediate if warranted

### 3.6 Annual FERPA Activities

#### 3.6.1 Annual FERPA Review

**When:** Annually, aligned with school year.

**Steps:**

1. **Review school official status**
   - For each school customer
   - Verify continued compliance

2. **Provide notification information**
   - Send to schools for annual FERPA notice
   - Offer template language

3. **Review disclosure log**
   - Audit for completeness
   - Address any gaps

4. **Review training records**
   - Ensure workforce FERPA training current

5. **Document review**
   - Record findings
   - Track any remediation

## 4. Escalation

Escalate to Legal/Compliance when:
- Unusual disclosure request
- Legal process received
- FERPA complaint received
- Uncertainty about compliance
