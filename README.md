# EdTech ISMS Policy and Procedure Framework

This repository contains a comprehensive set of policies and procedures for building a professional Information Security Management System (ISMS) specifically designed for **educational technology companies** handling student data. These documents provide audit-ready templates addressing FERPA, COPPA, state student privacy laws, and SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria.

## About This Framework

Building a robust EdTech compliance program requires comprehensive policies and procedures that satisfy multiple regulatory frameworks while remaining practical for implementation. This framework provides a complete ISMS designed specifically for organizations serving K-12 schools, school districts, and higher education institutions.

The documentation establishes student data governance, privacy compliance, security controls, and incident response capabilities that school districts, regulators, and auditors expect from EdTech vendors. Each document includes clear regulatory mappings and professional language suitable for procurement review.

## Who Is This For?

This policy framework is ideal for:
- **EdTech Companies** serving K-12 or higher education institutions
- **Organizations** processing student educational records under FERPA
- **Companies** serving children under 13 under COPPA
- **Vendors** responding to school district security questionnaires
- **Organizations** seeking SOC 2 certification with education-specific controls
- **Student Privacy Pledge** signatories demonstrating compliance commitments

## Compliance Coverage

This framework provides complete coverage of:

### Federal Education Privacy Laws
- **FERPA** (34 CFR Part 99): Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act
  - School official exception (34 CFR 99.31(a)(1))
  - Education records definitions and handling
  - Parent and eligible student rights
  - Re-disclosure limitations
- **COPPA** (16 CFR Part 312): Children's Online Privacy Protection Rule
  - School consent model for classroom use
  - Verifiable parental consent methods
  - Parent access and deletion rights

### State Student Privacy Laws
- **California**: SOPIPA (Student Online Personal Information Protection Act)
- **New York**: Education Law 2-d and 8 NYCRR Part 121
- **Colorado**: Student Data Transparency and Security Act (SB 16-158)
- **Connecticut**: Student Data Privacy Act (PA 16-189)
- **Illinois**: SOPPA (Student Online Personal Protection Act)

### Security Frameworks
- **SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria**: Common Criteria (CC1-CC8)
- **Student Privacy Pledge**: FPF/SIIA/CSTA commitments

## Key Features

- **School Official Exception Framework**: Complete implementation of FERPA school official criteria under 34 CFR 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B)
- **COPPA School Consent Model**: Procedures for schools to consent on behalf of parents for children under 13
- **State Law Matrix**: Requirements comparison for CA, NY, CO, CT, and IL
- **Data Processing Agreement Template**: Ready-to-use DPA aligned with SDPC standards
- **Breach Notification Procedures**: State-specific timelines and school notification workflows
- **Student Data Inventory**: Template for cataloging all student data elements
- **Parent Rights Notices**: FERPA/COPPA-compliant notice templates

## How to Use This Framework

**IMPORTANT:** These documents are **templates**, not a complete, out-of-the-box compliance solution. You must adapt them to fit your organization's specific technologies, processes, and risk tolerance.

1. **Clone or Fork this Repository:** Get a local copy to begin customizing.
2. **Find and Replace Placeholders:** Search for bracketed placeholders and replace them with your company's specific information. Common placeholders include:
    - `**[Company Name]**`
    - `**[Role Title, e.g., Privacy Officer]**`
    - `**[System Name, e.g., Learning Management System]**`
    - `**[Number, e.g., 30]**` (for specific timeframes like deletion windows)
3. **Review and Customize:** Read every policy and procedure carefully. Modify, add, or remove sections to accurately reflect how your organization operates.
4. **Use the Annexes:** Complete the FERPA control mapping, COPPA checklist, and state law matrix for your specific products.
5. **Expert Review:** Have the framework reviewed by qualified education privacy attorneys and compliance professionals before implementation.
6. **Formal Adoption:** Once approved, formally adopt the policies through your governance process and provide workforce training.
